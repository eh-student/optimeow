Feline Nutrition Essentials

Understanding your cat's nutritional needs for optimal health and longevity.

Understanding Feline Nutritional Needs

Cats are obligate carnivores, meaning they require a diet primarily composed of animal-based proteins. Unlike humans or dogs, cats have specific nutritional requirements that must be met through their diet.

Protein

Cats need high levels of animal protein (at least 30-40% of diet) for muscle maintenance and essential amino acids like taurine.

Fats

Essential fatty acids (20-30% of diet) provide energy and support skin/coat health. Cats need arachidonic acid from animal sources.

Minimal Carbs

Cats lack enzymes to process high carbs. Limited carbohydrates (<10%) are ideal to prevent obesity and diabetes.

Hydration is Critical

Cats naturally have low thirst drives, having evolved from desert-dwelling ancestors. Wet food can provide 70-80% of their water needs.

  • Always provide fresh, clean water multiple times daily
  • Consider a water fountain to encourage drinking
  • Mix water into dry food or switch to wet food for cats prone to urinary issues

Life Stage Nutrition

Kittens (0-12 months)

Higher protein/fat needs for growth (40-50% protein). Feed kitten-specific food and offer 3-4 small meals daily.

Adults (1-7 years)

Balanced diet for maintenance (30-40% protein). 2 meals daily. Monitor weight to prevent obesity.

Seniors (7+ years)

Easily digestible proteins, lower phosphorus. Watch for weight loss and adjust portions accordingly.

Feeding Best Practices

  • Establish consistent feeding times rather than free-feeding
  • Portion control is key - follow package guidelines or vet recommendations
  • Avoid sudden food changes - transition over 7-10 days
  • Warm wet food slightly to enhance aroma (never microwave - stir test temperature)
  • Use puzzle feeders to stimulate natural hunting behaviors
  • Clean food bowls daily and provide fresh water at all times
